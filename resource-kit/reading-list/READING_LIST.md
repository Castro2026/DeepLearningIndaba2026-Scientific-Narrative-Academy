# Curated & Annotated Reading List

*Mastering the Scientific Narrative — Resource Kit.*
Each entry says **why it earns your time** and **which pass it deserves** (see the 3-Pass method).

---

## A. Reading papers

1. **S. Keshav — “How to Read a Paper” (ACM SIGCOMM CCR, 2007).**
   Two pages that will save you two hundred hours: the original 3-Pass method taught in the session. → *Pass 2 tonight; re-read every year.*
   Search: `Keshav how to read a paper pdf` (freely available from the author's University of Waterloo page).

## B. Writing papers

2. **B. Mensh & K. Kording — “Ten simple rules for structuring papers” (PLOS Comput. Biol., 2017).**
   The best short guide to paper-as-story: one paper = one contribution; context–content–conclusion at every scale. Open access. → *Pass 2 before your first draft.*
3. **G. Gopen & J. Swan — “The Science of Scientific Writing” (American Scientist, 1990).**
   Why readers misunderstand grammatically correct sentences — and how to place information where readers expect it. → *Pass 2 while polishing.*
4. **G. Whitesides — “Whitesides' Group: Writing a Paper” (Advanced Materials, 2004).**
   The outline-first culture of a hyper-productive lab: the paper begins on day 1 of the project, not after the experiments. → *Pass 1, adopt the habit.*
5. **The Heilmeier Catechism (DARPA).**
   Nine questions used to vet research programs; questions 1–4 generate your Introduction. Search: `Heilmeier catechism darpa`. → *Answer in writing before any new project.*

## C. Figures, data & reproducibility

6. **N. Rougier, M. Droettboom, P. Bourne — “Ten Simple Rules for Better Figures” (PLOS Comput. Biol., 2014).**
   Open access, with matplotlib examples; pairs directly with the figure checklist. → *Pass 2 before making your figures.*
7. **M. Wilkinson et al. — “The FAIR Guiding Principles…” (Scientific Data, 2016).**
   The founding paper of Findable–Accessible–Interoperable–Reusable data; cite it when you release an African dataset. → *Pass 1; keep as reference.*
8. **T. Gebru et al. — “Datasheets for Datasets” (CACM, 2021).**
   The dataset-card standard: the questions your dataset biography must answer (provenance, consent, biases, license). → *Use as a fill-in template.*
9. **NeurIPS Paper Checklist (neurips.cc).**
   The official reproducibility & ethics questions attached to every submission — read them **before** running experiments, not after. → *Pass 1 at project start.*

## D. Scientific integrity & style in ML

10. **Z. Lipton & J. Steinhardt — “Troubling Trends in Machine Learning Scholarship” (2018, arXiv).**
    The four sins reviewers are primed to catch: speculation-as-explanation, overclaiming ("suggestive" language), mathiness, poor empirical rigor. → *Pass 2; audit your own draft against it.*

## E. African AI research & community

11. **Masakhane (∀, W. Nekoto, V. Marivate et al.) — “Participatory Research for Low-resourced Machine Translation” (Findings of EMNLP, 2020).**
    Proof that a distributed African grassroots collective can publish at a top venue — and a model for participatory data collection. → *Pass 2; then join masakhane.io.*
12. **Deep Learning Indaba (deeplearningindaba.com).** The continental gathering; its workshops accept first papers and its mentorship programmes are outstanding.
13. **AfricaNLP workshop (co-located with major *CL conferences).** The natural first submission target for language work — real reviews, kind stakes.
14. **Zindi (zindi.africa).** African data-science competitions: a source of datasets, co-authors, and results that can seed a workshop paper.

## F. Tools (set these up this week)

- **Zotero / Mendeley** — reference manager; the Zotero browser connector turns every Pass-1 into a saved, tagged record.
- **Overleaf** — collaborative LaTeX; official venue templates in the gallery (see `templates/overleaf/`).
- **Semantic Scholar & Connected Papers** — map a literature from one seed paper (feeds your Related Work).
- **Elicit / Scite** — AI-assisted discovery. *Servants, not masters:* they find papers; Pass-2/3 judgement stays yours.
- **Zenodo (zenodo.org)** — free DOIs to archive datasets and code (FAIR, principle F).
- **HuggingFace Hub** — host datasets/models with cards; where African corpora become visible infrastructure.

### Suggested 4-week starter plan
**W1:** items 1, 5 · set up Zotero + Overleaf. **W2:** items 2, 6 · draft your 3-sentence abstract into a title + intro skeleton. **W3:** items 7, 8 · write your dataset biography. **W4:** items 10, 11 · Pass-1 five papers from your target venue's last edition.
