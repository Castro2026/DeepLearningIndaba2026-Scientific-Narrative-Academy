# Contributing

Thanks for your interest in improving this workshop! This repository hosts
the material for the Deep Learning Indaba 2026 Skill Session *"Mastering the
Scientific Narrative"*, and contributions from past/future participants and
fellow facilitators are welcome.

## Ways to contribute

- **Fix errors or typos** in the slides, docs, or templates.
- **Improve the Resource Kit** — checklists, scenario cards, reading list,
  reference-manager guides.
- **Add examples** of good/weak abstracts or introductions (`examples/`),
  as long as they are anonymized or your own work.
- **Extend venue templates** (`templates/`) for additional conferences or
  journals.
- **Translate** material into other languages relevant to the audience.
- **Report issues** with broken links, outdated references, or unclear
  instructions.

## How to propose a change

1. Fork the repository and create a branch from `main`.
2. Make your change, keeping the folder structure described in the
   [README](README.md#repository-structure).
3. Keep binary files (`.pptx`, `.docx`, `.pdf`) in sync with any source you
   also edit (e.g. the LaTeX template in `resource-kit/overleaf/`).
4. Open a pull request describing what changed and why.

## Style notes

- Keep new documents consistent with the existing tone: concrete, numbersdriven, and grounded in African AI research contexts.
- Cite African research where relevant — one of the goals of this session is
  to make that literature more visible.
- Do not invent citations, datasets, or results in examples — mark clearly
  invented placeholder numbers (e.g. in the abstract exercise) as such.

## Questions

For anything beyond a straightforward fix, reach out to the facilitator at
castro.hounmenou@univ-labe.edu.gn before starting significant work.
